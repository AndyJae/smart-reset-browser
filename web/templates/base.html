smart-reset Help

smart-reset is designed to return all color and black values to their original settings. After all values have been reset, the camera is ready for White and Black Balance.

Scan Network — finds cameras in your network. Detected cameras are listed in the scan dropdown.

Connect — establishes a connection. If the IP field is empty and scan results are available, Connect automatically uses the first discovered camera. You can still enter IP/port manually at any time.

Reset Camera — runs the full reset sequence. The button is active only when connected. Advanced controls are loaded based on the detected camera model. Unsupported functions are disabled automatically.

Operation details are shown in the log panel (and optional file log).

After reset, run ABB / AWW as needed and activate the options you want to use.

To open the camera WebGUI directly, use the Open Camera button in the sidebar. Some values may require a WebGUI refresh before they are visible there.

NDI Monitor

The NDI Monitor displays a live video feed, waveform, and vectorscope directly in the browser. NDI output must be enabled on the camera.

Link with camera — when checked, the NDI feed starts automatically when the camera connects and stops when it disconnects. The tool refreshes the source list and selects the matching feed by camera IP. The dropdown remains usable at all times.

Refresh — scans the network for NDI sources. The list is not updated automatically — press Refresh whenever a new camera or NDI source becomes available.

Start — opens the NDI connection. After clicking Start, the image takes 3–4 seconds to appear. This is normal: NDI needs a short negotiation phase before the first frame is delivered.

Click any image to open it fullscreen. The waveform mode (RGB Parade, RGB Overlay, Luma) can be changed at any time without reconnecting.

Waveform — shows signal levels per channel. All modes are computed in Rec. 709 colour space. Graticule lines at 0, 25, 50, 75, and 100 IRE.

Vectorscope — YCbCr Rec. 709 trace with saturation rings every 10 %, a crosshair at the neutral point, and 75 % colour-bar target boxes (Y, Cy, G, Mg, R, B). Use it to verify colour accuracy and white balance on the connected camera.
